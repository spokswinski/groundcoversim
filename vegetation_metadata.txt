ABSTRACT:				
Longleaf pine ecosystems are rich in plant species and represent the dominant upland forest type in several southeastern military bases. In order to manage these forests to fulfill military objectives and to conserve this unique natural resources, we investigated mechanisms of diversity and neutral theory. We coupled a series of field experiments with data mining exercises to help managers meet their objectives for monitoring the impact of various activities on the understory plant community. The data collected from these experiments will then go towards the development of modeling tools to help evaluate different management techniques based on links between overstory structure, fire and understory plant diversity. Vegetation sampling techniques and data are critical components to link forest structure measured by remote sensing to interaction diversity and to evaluate the neutral theory of biological diversity as a possible mechanism for high diversity in areas of frequent disturbance. In this case, by fire. Research took place on Eglin AFB, FL from fall 2012 through fall 2016. All groundcover vegetation sampling plots were burned during the springs of 2013 and 2015. Vegetation sampling occurred in the monitoring plots every fall. 			
				
METHODS: 				
Plot selection:	Monitoring plot locations were co-located with Jackson Guard monitoring plots listed as high quality sandhills and flatwoods that were scheduled to burn in FY2012 (contact Brett Williams, Wildland Fire Manager at Jackson Guard for JG monitoring data and methods). Using a stem map of the overstory in the JG monitoring plots, a 4 m buffer was placed around all trees using ArcGIS. Using these buffers, three layers were produced representing three canopy types: tree clumps (trees that had overlapping buffers), single tree (buffers that did not overlap) and open (areas in the monitoring plot that were not under overstory canopy). Ten points were randomly generated in each monitoring plot under each canopy type and loaded into a Trimble Juno SA running ArcMap. Using the Trimble unit to navigate to each point, one quadrat was selected from the 10 points for each canopy type starting with the first point until a suitable point was reached. Quadrats are recorded as single points in the Quad_locations.shp file. Fire exclusion plots were selected close to the edge of the sandhill burn units that could be easily accessed and defended from fire. Fuel manipulation plots were selected under single trees that were three separate burn units scheduled to be burned in that year. Individual quadrats are represented by single points found in the northwest corner of the center square of the 1m X 3m quadrat. Scale diversity plots were selected from suitable sandhill sites located across Eglin AFB reservation with various time since fire and 20 year burn histories. 
			

GROUNDCOVER SAMPLING PLOTS: 
Groundcover sampling was done in 1m X 3m quadrats. Vegetation monitoring plots had three quadrats per plot. Plots were co-located with JG monitoring plots established 2003-2006. Five sandhill plots and five Flatwoods plots were selected for a total of 30 monitoring quadrats. Quadrats were sampled at a 100 cm2 resolution. Quadrats were established and sampled using a Hornsby square. A Hornsby square is a rigid aluminum frame with inside dimensions of 1m X 1m. Along the upper edge of the square, grooves are cut every ten centimeters so that 3 mm stainless steel rods can be placed in a grid pattern. The outside portion of the square has four vertical sleeves at the corners that are lined up with two inside planes of the square. When establishing the quadrat, four « inch stainless steel rods are driven through the sleeves. The Hornsby square can be placed on two of the rods then shifted twice to create a 10 x 300 cell grid with a 100 cm2 resolution. Sampling began in the lower southwest corner of the quadrat moving east through the row and then to the next row north. Presence was recorded for each species found in an individual cell. In sampling years 2012-2015, location in that cell by quadrant (NW, NE, SW, SE) that contained each species was recorded.

DATA ANOMALIES:				
Sandhill block 3 was burned out of sequence and no fire intensity data was collected throughout the project.				
Quadrat FOPEN5 sustained coyote/hog damage through its southern half prior to 2015 sampling. 								
It was determined that a 100 cm2 resolution would be sufficient and sub-cell sampling was discontinued after 2015.				
				
				
DATA ENTRY:				
Location of raw data:	N/A			
Data entry and QA/QC:	Data was entered into Microsoft Excel from hardcopies of recorded data, each on a separate worksheet. The worksheets were compared on a third worksheet and hardcopies were referenced for changes. Once all corrections were made, a final worksheet was transposed and added to the master vegetation file. Beginning in 2013, copies of the previous years data were taken into the field to check for accuracy. Additionally, all data was copied to a separate worksheet following initial entry and species code data was sorted alphabetically. Any misspelled species codes were corrected in the master file.
